# The Hidden Cost of Shipping Everything in Rune Factory

## Overview

During long-term play, I realized that shipping isn't just about making money.

Every new crop or flower you ship may expand the shop's inventory pool, while the number of display slots remains limited.

That completely changed how I manage my farm.

I now think of shipping as managing my future shop inventory rather than simply selling items for profit.

---

## Key Takeaway

Shipping every crop or flower is not always the best long-term strategy.

As the inventory candidate pool grows, the chance of seeing a specific desired item may gradually decrease because the number of shop display slots remains limited.

This article introduces one possible optimization strategy based on repeated gameplay observations.

## Practical Implications

This idea is especially useful for players who enjoy long-term optimization.

For example:

- Keep frequently purchased crops available in shops.
- Think carefully before shipping rarely used crops or flowers.
- Fruit trees are often a special case because they provide renewable harvests once fully grown.
- Shop inventory management can reduce preparation time for cooking, crafting, and friendship farming.

Over a long playthrough, deciding **what not to ship** can sometimes be just as important as deciding what to ship.

## Notes

This article is based on repeated gameplay observations and long-term play experience.

It does **not** claim to describe the internal game code.

Different playstyles may benefit from different strategies, and there is no single "correct" way to play.
