I think I may have figured out why Mealy Apple inheritance becomes so unreliable



本文
After observing inheritance behavior in RF4S/RF5, I noticed something interesting.
Adding more Glitter Augite creates more successful inheritance combinations.
Adding different materials only increases the candidate pool.
This can reduce the success rate from 57.1% to 11.4%.
In early follow-up tests, a setup using six Glitter Augites succeeded 4 times out of 5 in RF5 and 3 times out of 5 in RF4S, while a conventional three-Glitter setup succeeded only once in 5 attempts in both games.
I later expanded the testing to 1,000 attempts for each setup (4,000 total attempts). The observed success rates remained surprisingly close to the combinatorial model.
RF5 data currently includes 3-, 5-, and 6-Glitter setups; a 4-Glitter test was not completed due to limited Glitter Augite availability.



For anyone interested, I documented the entire investigation, including additional observations and unresolved questions:
